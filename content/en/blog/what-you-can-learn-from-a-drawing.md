When you look at your child's drawing, you might see a house, a sun, and a stick figure. With
a little practice, you can see a lot more — and none of it requires guessing at hidden meanings.

## Seven things a drawing shows

- **Creativity & imagination** — unusual choices, going beyond the template.
- **Color & light** — how colors are chosen, combined, and applied.
- **Composition & space** — how the page is used and how elements hold together.
- **Technique & materials** — control of the pencil, marker, or paint.
- **Fine motor skills & detail** — small, precise, repeatable marks.
- **Form & proportions** — how the shape of things is rendered.
- **Story & interaction** — whether there's a narrative or characters.

Each of these is **visible**. You're not inferring feelings — you're noticing what the hand and
eye did on the page, against what's typical for the child's age.

## From noticing to supporting

The point isn't to grade your child. It's to notice a strength and offer a next step. Saw a
confident, large figure? Try a big sheet and "draw it in one line." Lots of careful little
patterns? Offer borders and decorative motifs. Only one character and no scene? Suggest a story
where the character meets someone.

Small, specific, ten-minute invitations — that's how a quiet observation turns into real support.
